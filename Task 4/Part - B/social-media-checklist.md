# Social Media Do's and Don'ts
**Digital Literacy | Task 4 | Prateek Tripathi | 25BAI11598**

---

## ✅ 5 DO'S

1. **Use privacy settings deliberately**  
   Review and configure privacy settings on personal accounts. Control exactly who can see your content, your contact details, and your activity. Do not leave defaults unchecked.

2. **Share professional milestones publicly**  
   Post achievements, completed projects, certifications, and learning milestones on LinkedIn and GitHub. These create a public record of growth that serves as a portfolio.

3. **Think before posting — apply the 3-year rule**  
   Before posting anything, ask: *Would I want a recruiter, professor, or future employer to see this in 3 years?* If the answer is uncertain, do not post it.

4. **Credit others' work when sharing**  
   When resharing or building on someone else's content, code, or ideas, explicitly credit the original creator. Attribution is a professional standard, not optional courtesy.

5. **Audit your digital footprint regularly**  
   Search your own name periodically. Check what images, comments, and profiles appear. Know your public profile before a recruiter does.

---

## ❌ 5 DON'TS

1. **Don't post when emotional**  
   Never post, comment, or reply when upset, angry, or tired. Impulsive content on social media is permanent. The platform remembers even after you delete it.

2. **Don't share sensitive personal information**  
   Never post your phone number, home address, Aadhaar number, UPI ID, or daily location publicly. This information enables identity theft, fraud, and physical risk.

3. **Don't engage in public controversies under your real name**  
   Avoid getting drawn into political arguments, personal disputes, or controversial debates on public platforms using your real identity. It follows you professionally.

4. **Don't carry informal tone into professional platforms**  
   The communication style used on WhatsApp or Instagram is not appropriate on LinkedIn, email, or professional forums. Adapt your tone to the platform and the audience.

5. **Don't click, share, or spread unverified information**  
   Before resharing any news, statistic, or claim, verify it through a credible source. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — damages credibility and harms others.

## Reflection 

Writing this checklist made me realise how rarely I apply these rules in
practice, even when I know them in theory. The 3-year rule for posting is
something I had never consciously thought about before. It is a simple mental
filter that immediately changes how you evaluate content before sharing it.

The don't about informal tone is the one most students violate most
consistently — including me. The habit of casual, abbreviated communication
is so deeply ingrained from messaging apps that it bleeds into emails,
LinkedIn messages, and even academic submissions without us noticing.

The most important insight: digital professionalism is not about following
rules. It is about developing habits that protect and build your reputation
automatically, without having to think consciously about each decision.
