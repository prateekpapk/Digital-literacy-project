# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — use a password manager (Bitwarden, Google Password Manager) rather than reusing the same password across platforms.
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps. Even if your password leaks, 2FA blocks unauthorised access.
- [ ] **Think before you click** — do not open links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders, even if they appear to be from your bank, college, or a delivery service.
- [ ] **Keep your apps and phone OS updated** — security patches fix vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.
- [ ] **Never install apps from outside the official App Store or Google Play** — APK files from links are a common method of installing malware.
- [ ] **Review app permissions** — a flashlight app does not need access to your contacts or microphone. Revoke unnecessary permissions in your phone settings.

---

## 💳 Financial / UPI Safety 

- [ ] **A payment REQUEST requires your PIN — receiving money does NOT.** If someone tells you to "approve" or "confirm" an incoming payment by entering your PIN, it is a scam. You only enter your UPI PIN to *send* money.
- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not with customer care, not with bank representatives, not with friends. Legitimate institutions never ask for these details over a call or message.
- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your bank app — this caps your maximum possible loss if your account is ever compromised.
- [ ] **Verify the recipient's name before every transfer** — UPI shows the registered account name before you confirm. Always check it matches who you intend to pay.

## 📢 Reporting Channels

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file complaints online for financial fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, and more |
| **Helpline 1930** | National Cybercrime Helpline — call immediately after financial fraud; early reporting freezes transactions |
| **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Every major bank has a 24/7 fraud reporting line — save yours in your contacts now, before you need it |

---

> ⚡ **If you are scammed: call 1930 first, then visit cybercrime.gov.in. Every minute matters.**
